The Problem

Contracts are risky by default

Most people sign without truly understanding the legal exposure hidden in plain text.

📄
Dense legal language
Contracts use opaque terminology, nested conditions, and buried exceptions that non-lawyers miss.
💸
Legal review is expensive
Attorney review costs $300–$500/hr. Freelancers, startups, and individuals can't afford it for every contract.
⏱️
Time pressure
Counterparties push for quick signatures. There's rarely enough time to read every clause carefully.
⚖️
Hidden risk clauses
Non-compete, IP assignment, and auto-renewal clauses can have serious consequences when overlooked.

Core Features

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

🔍
Clause Extraction
Detects 13 legal clause types: liability, IP assignment, non-compete, auto-renewal, and more — with confidence scores.
⚖️
Risk Classification
Chain-of-thought reasoning assigns Low / Medium / High risk to each clause with a 0–1 risk score and full explanation.
📚
Precedent Retrieval
Hybrid RAG (dense + BM25) searches a legal knowledge base for relevant precedents to ground every recommendation.
💡
Plain-Language Recs
Generates human-readable explanations and suggested alternative clause language — ready to negotiate with.

Engineering Challenges

Real problems. Concrete fixes.

Challenge
Free-tier rate limits
Groq's 6,000 TPM cap caused 429 errors when classifying clauses in parallel.
✓ Fix
Configurable LLM_REQUEST_DELAY with sequential processing. Zero delay for local Ollama.
Challenge
Inconsistent LLM output
LLMs returned reasoning as arrays, invented clause type names, or produced malformed JSON.
✓ Fix
Pydantic v2 field validators coerce lists → strings and unknown enums → OTHER. Max 2 retries with error feedback.
Challenge
Orchestrator data flow bug
Risk classifier and precedent retriever ran in parallel — but the retriever needed classified clauses (with risk_level) first.
✓ Fix
Refactored LangGraph pipeline to classify first, then retrieve precedents sequentially using classified output.
